\section{Literature Referenced}
\subsection{If the reference is a journal or conference paper}
Template for title should be like \\ First Author Name et al. (year) - Paper or conference title

The body contains Research Objective, Methodology Used,  Key Findings, Strengths \& Contributions, 6. Limitations \& Research Gaps, Relevance to your Project

Example: 
Smith et al. (2022) - IoT-Based Smart Energy Meter for Real-Time Monitoring
The integration of IoT in energy metering has been a growing field of research, aiming
to enhance monitoring and efficiency in electricity consumption. Several earlier projects,
such as the IoT DC Energy Meter and the GSM Prepaid Energy Meter, attempted to au-
tomate energy measurement. However, traditional methods still required manual reading
of meters, making the process time-consuming and inefficient.
\\
A recent study introduced an IoT-based Electricity Energy Meter using ESP32 and the
Blynk application, providing a real-time, remote monitoring solution[1]. This approach
automates electricity consumption tracking and offers a cost-effective and time-efficient
alternative to traditional methods. \\ The project incorporates current and voltage sensors to measure power consumption accurately. The SCT-013 Non-Invasive AC Current Sensor
is selected for its capability to measure AC currents up to 100A, while the ZMPT101B
AC Voltage Sensor Module ensures precise voltage readings. The collected data is then
transmitted to the Blynk dashboard, allowing users to monitor voltage, current, power,
and total units consumed in real-time.
Despite its advantages, the IoT-based system has certain limitations. The depen-
dency on stable internet connectivity poses challenges in areas with network instability.
Additionally, sensor calibration is crucial for ensuring accuracy, making the initial setup
process demanding. \\
Overall, IoT-based smart energy meters present a significant advancement in energy
monitoring, enabling automated data collection and remote access. Future research could
focus on enhancing data reliability in low-connectivity regions and improving sensor cal-
ibration techniques to optimize accuracy.
